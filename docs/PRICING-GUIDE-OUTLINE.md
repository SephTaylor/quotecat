# The Contractor Pricing Guide

**Subtitle:** How to Price Jobs Profitably, Stop Leaving Money on the Table, and Keep More of What You Earn

**Target audience:** Residential contractors (electricians, plumbers, HVAC, remodelers, painters, general contractors)

**Price:** $29-49

**Format:** PDF, ~40-50 pages

## Outline

### Introduction: Why Most Contractors Underprice (and How to Fix It)
- The "I just want to win the job" trap
- Why racing to the bottom kills your business
- The goal: charge what you're worth AND win jobs

### Part 1: Know Your Numbers

**Chapter 1: Your Real Costs (They're Higher Than You Think)**
- Direct costs: materials, labor, subcontractors
- Indirect costs: truck, tools, insurance, licenses
- Hidden costs: callbacks, warranty work, unbilled time
- Worksheet: Calculate your true hourly cost to operate

**Chapter 2: Setting Your Labor Rate**
- The $50/hour myth (why most contractors charge too little)
- Formula: True cost + profit margin = billable rate
- Hourly vs day rate vs project rate - when to use each
- Regional rate comparison (what others charge)

**Chapter 3: Markup vs Margin (Know the Difference)**
- Markup: what you add to cost
- Margin: what you keep as profit
- The math: why 30% markup ≠ 30% profit
- Recommended markups by trade
- Worksheet: Find your target markup

---

### Part 2: Pricing Strategies That Win Jobs

**Chapter 4: The Good/Better/Best Framework**
- Why one price = take it or leave it
- Three tiers = client chooses, you always win
- How to structure tiers (not just "more stuff")
- Real examples by trade:
  - Electrical: panel upgrade tiers
  - Plumbing: water heater replacement tiers
  - HVAC: system replacement tiers
  - Remodel: kitchen renovation tiers

**Chapter 5: Flat Rate vs Time & Materials**
- When flat rate wins (predictable jobs)
- When T&M wins (unknowns, older homes)
- Hybrid approach: flat rate + T&M for discoveries
- How to present T&M without scaring clients

**Chapter 6: The Psychology of Pricing**
- Anchoring: why you show the big number first
- Charm pricing ($4,997 vs $5,000) - does it work?
- The decoy effect in tiered pricing
- Confidence: how you present the price matters more than the number

**Chapter 9: Handling Price Objections**
